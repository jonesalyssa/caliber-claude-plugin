# Caliber QA

Use this framework before approving any political mailer, digital ad, social graphic, palm card, door hanger, website graphic, fundraising piece, or campaign creative.

The objective is simple:

**Catch problems before the client, voter, printer, reporter, or opponent does.**

Every piece should pass strategic, creative, factual, and production review.

---

# Core Rule

Never ask:

"Does this look good?"

Ask:

"Does this accomplish the objective?"

A beautiful piece that fails persuasion is still a failed piece.

---

# Strategic QA

Before reviewing design:

Confirm the strategy.

---

## Objective Check

Can you identify the objective in one sentence?

Examples:

- Elect Candidate X
- Defeat Candidate Y
- Increase turnout
- Build trust
- Raise concerns
- Introduce candidate

If the objective is unclear, the piece is not ready.

---

## Message Check

What is the single takeaway?

A voter should remember one thing.

Not five.

Not ten.

One.

If multiple messages compete, simplify.

---

## Audience Check

Is the piece designed for the intended audience?

Examples:

- Republicans
- Democrats
- Independents
- Seniors
- Parents
- Veterans
- Hispanic voters
- Rural voters

A message for activists may fail with swing voters.

---

# Three-Second Test

Look at the piece for three seconds.

Then ask:

Who is this about?

Is it positive or negative?

What is the message?

If any answer is unclear:

The design failed.

---

# Headline QA

Check:

- Is the headline dominant?
- Is it readable from arm's length?
- Is it emotional?
- Is it memorable?
- Is it specific?
- Is it shorter than 12 words?

Strong headlines usually win.

Weak headlines usually sink pieces.

---

# Copy QA

Review every sentence.

Ask:

Can this be shorter?

Can this be clearer?

Can this be stronger?

---

## Compression Test

Remove:

- filler
- repetition
- consultant language
- jargon

Every word must earn its place.

---

## Bullet Test

Bullets should:

- Be short
- Be parallel
- Be scannable
- Have equal visual weight

Ideal:

Three bullets.

Consistent length.

Consistent structure.

---

# Contrast QA

Identify:

Hero

Villain

Conflict

Consequence

If any are unclear, strengthen contrast.

---

## Contrast Questions

Can voters tell:

Who to support?

Who to oppose?

Why it matters?

Within seconds?

If not, improve contrast.

---

# Visual QA

Ask:

Does the visual support the argument?

Or merely decorate the page?

Every major image should reinforce messaging.

---

# Concept QA

Can the visual metaphor be explained in one sentence?

Examples:

Evidence board = corruption

Burning money = waste

Warning label = danger

Community photo = trust

If the concept requires explanation, it is too weak.

---

# Layout QA

Review:

- Alignment
- Spacing
- Balance
- Hierarchy

Check for:

- Crowding
- Floating elements
- Uneven spacing
- Competing focal points

If everything is important, nothing is important.

---

# Readability QA

Body copy minimum:

12pt

Check:

- Contrast
- Font size
- Line spacing
- Reverse type

Senior voters should be able to read the piece comfortably.

---

## Reverse Type Rule

Avoid:

White body copy on dark backgrounds.

Especially:

Small text.

Sources.

Bullets.

Captions.

Use dark text on light backgrounds whenever possible.

---

# Photo QA

Positive photos should feel:

- Warm
- Confident
- Human
- Approachable

Negative photos should feel:

- Serious
- Credible
- Appropriate to the argument

Never:

- Distort faces
- Over-edit faces
- Create unrealistic imagery

---

# Fact Check QA

For every major claim ask:

What is the source?

Can we produce it immediately?

Would a reporter accept it?

Would opposing counsel challenge it?

If uncertain:

Rewrite or source more aggressively.

---

# Statistics QA

Verify:

- Date
- Geography
- Methodology
- Original source

Outdated statistics weaken credibility.

---

# Quote QA

Confirm:

- Exact wording
- Proper attribution
- Context

Never rely on memory.

---

# Production QA

Review technical requirements.

---

## Trim Safety

Check:

- No important text near trim
- No faces near trim
- No logos near trim
- No QR codes near trim

Maintain safety margins.

---

## Bleed

Confirm:

Bleeds extend properly.

No accidental white edges.

---

## Resolution

Images should be:

300 DPI minimum for print.

Avoid:

- Pixelation
- Compression artifacts
- AI distortions

---

## Color

Confirm:

- Consistent palette
- Proper contrast
- Print-safe colors

Dark prints often reproduce darker than expected.

Compensate accordingly.

---

# Client QA

Ask:

Does this match the campaign brand?

Does this match previous pieces?

Does this fit the candidate?

Consistency matters.

---

# Competitive QA

Imagine this piece in a stack of mail.

Ask:

Would voters notice it?

Would voters remember it?

Would it stand apart from competitors?

If not:

Strengthen the concept.

---

# "More Contrast" QA

When someone requests more contrast:

Identify the actual issue.

Possible problems:

- Weak headline
- Weak hierarchy
- Weak visual metaphor
- Weak color separation
- Weak emotional difference
- Weak candidate/opponent distinction

Fix the root problem.

Not the symptom.

---

# Red Flag Checklist

Immediately review if you see:

- Tiny body copy
- Busy backgrounds
- More than two fonts
- Too many colors
- Long headlines
- Paragraph bullets
- Weak contrast
- Generic stock photos
- Decorative clutter
- Unsourced attacks

These are common failure points.

---

# Final Approval Questions

Before approval ask:

Can voters understand the message in three seconds?

Is the headline dominant?

Is the contrast obvious?

Is the copy concise?

Is the visual persuasive?

Are claims sourced?

Is the piece readable?

Will it print correctly?

Does it stand out?

Would you confidently defend every claim publicly?

If any answer is no:

Continue revising.

---

# Approval Standard

A piece is ready when:

The strategy is clear.

The message is memorable.

The facts are defensible.

The design is readable.

The concept is persuasive.

Everything else is secondary.

# Production QA

Review all technical requirements before sending to print.

---

## Document Setup

### Bleed

All backgrounds, photos, and color fills that touch the edge must extend **0.125" beyond trim** on all sides.

No accidental white edges.

If it bleeds, it needs 0.125".

### Safety Margin

Keep all critical content — text, logos, faces, QR codes, disclaimers — **at least 0.125" inside trim**.

Preferred: 0.1875" (3/16") inside trim for body copy and headlines.

Nothing important within 0.125" of any edge.

### Fold Lines (self-mailers)

No text, face, or logo should cross a fold line.

Confirm fold placement matches the printer's spec for that piece size.

Allow at least 0.125" clearance on each side of every fold.

## USPS Compliance

### Indicia Placement

Standard mail indicia: **top right corner of mailing panel**.

Minimum clear area around indicia: 0.25" on all sides.

No text, image, or color behind the indicia box.

### Mailing Label / IMb Barcode

Place in the **address block area**, lower right of mailing panel.

Minimum clear zone below address block: 0.625" from bottom edge (USPS optical character reader zone).

No ink, texture, or photo in the barcode clear zone.

### Return Address

Upper left of mailing panel.

Required on all standard and nonprofit mail.

### EDDM-Specific

Must include "ECRWSS EDDM" in place of a specific address.

Retail EDDM: indicia reads "US POSTAGE PAID / EDDM RETAIL".

Bundling tabs required at printer — confirm with production.

### Tab/Wafer Seal Requirements (self-mailers)

USPS requires self-mailers to be tabbed or glued closed on the open edge.

Standard: 1–2 wafer seals on the open side, positioned per USPS DMM guidelines.

Confirm tab placement with printer before finalizing layout — seal placement affects design near edges.

---

## Disclaimer / Legal Requirements

### "Paid For By" Statement

**Required on every piece.**

Must include the full committee name as registered with the relevant authority (FEC, state, or local).

Example: _Paid for by Citizens for Jane Smith_

### Placement

Bottom of mailing panel, or bottom of primary face panel.

Never bury in a color field that reduces legibility.

### Size

Minimum **7pt** for most state requirements.

FEC: no mandated size, but must be "clear and conspicuous."

When in doubt: **8pt minimum**, high-contrast, readable.

### Disclaimer for Attack Mail

If the piece attacks an opponent, confirm whether state law requires additional disclosure language.

Some states require: _"[Candidate] did not approve this message"_ or equivalent.

Check state-specific requirements for every race.

---

## Color

### File Mode

All print files must be submitted in **CMYK**.

RGB files will shift at press — blues go purple, reds go muddy.

Convert to CMYK before final export. Check for color shift after conversion.

### Rich Black

For large black areas, use rich black: **C60 M40 Y40 K100**.

Do not use rich black on small text — causes registration blur.

Small black text: **K100 only**.

### Saturated Colors

Bright reds, blues, and greens shift darker in print.

Proof on a calibrated monitor or request a press proof for hero colors.

Total ink coverage should not exceed **300% CMYK** (some printers: 280%).

### Coating

Gloss coating: intensifies color, reduces readability on small text.

Matte/silk: softer, easier to read, less pop on photos.

Confirm coating choice with account manager before file prep — coating affects color build.

---

## Resolution

Images: **300 DPI minimum at final print size**.

Logos: submit as vector (`.ai`, `.eps`, `.pdf`) whenever possible.

Never scale a raster image above 100% of its original size.

Avoid screenshots, web-pulled images, and social media exports — these are 72–96 DPI and will not print cleanly.

---

## File Submission

Submit as **press-ready PDF** with:

- Bleed included
- Crop marks (offset 0.125")
- All fonts embedded or outlined
- CMYK color mode
- No spot colors unless confirmed with printer
- Layers flattened

Confirm file specs with printer before first submission on any new format.
