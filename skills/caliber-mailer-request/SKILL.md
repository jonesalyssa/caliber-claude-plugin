# Caliber Mailer Request

Use this framework whenever a user requests:

- A political mailer
- Mail concept development
- Creative direction
- Direct mail strategy
- Positive mail
- Contrast mail
- Attack mail
- Comparison mail
- Palm cards
- Door hangers
- Digital ad concepts
- Political design recommendations

Never begin designing until all required information has been collected.

---

# Objective

The goal is to gather enough information to produce:

1. Strong messaging
2. Effective contrast
3. Appropriate creative direction
4. Readable layouts
5. Accurate factual claims
6. Distinct visual concepts

The better the inputs, the better the mail piece.

---

# Required Information

Collect the following information whenever possible.

---

## Candidate

Candidate Name:

Party:

Office Sought:

District:

Incumbent or Challenger:

Election Date:

---

## Piece Type

What are we creating?

- Positive mail
- Negative mail
- Comparison mail
- GOTV
- Absentee
- Introduction
- Contrast
- Endorsement
- Fundraising
- Digital ad
- Palm card
- Door hanger

---

## Objective

What should the voter do?

Examples:

- Vote for candidate
- Vote against opponent
- Request ballot
- Turn out to vote
- Learn candidate
- Remember candidate
- Distrust opponent

Every piece should have one primary objective.

---

## Audience

Who is receiving the piece?

Examples:

- Republicans
- Democrats
- Independents
- Swing voters
- Parents
- Seniors
- Veterans
- Hispanic voters
- Rural voters
- Suburban voters

Different audiences require different messaging.

---

## Core Message

What is the single most important thing voters should remember?

Examples:

- Proven conservative fighter
- Lower taxes
- Public safety
- Border security
- Corruption concerns
- Failed leadership
- Parent rights
- Economic growth

One message is better than five messages.

---

## Supporting Points

Provide:

2–5 supporting points.

Examples:

- Protected women's sports
- Lowered taxes
- Opposed wasteful spending
- Increased law enforcement funding
- Backed parental rights

These become bullets and supporting copy.

---

## Sources

Provide all available sources.

Examples:

- Voting records
- Public statements
- News articles
- Campaign websites
- Legislative records
- Financial disclosures

Every major claim should be sourced.

---

# Creative Direction Questions

---

## Positive or Negative

Should the piece feel:

- Positive
- Negative
- Contrast
- Mixed

This determines tone and visual treatment.

---

## Desired Emotion

What should voters feel?

Examples:

- Trust
- Confidence
- Pride
- Hope
- Concern
- Frustration
- Anger
- Urgency

Emotion drives creative direction.

---

## Visual Theme

If known, provide preferred themes.

Examples:

- Western
- Rural
- Evidence board
- Newspaper
- Surveillance
- Corruption
- Financial
- Public safety
- Patriotic
- Local community

If no theme is provided, recommend concepts based on the argument.

---

## Existing Brand Elements

Provide:

- Logo
- Fonts
- Color palette
- Existing mail examples
- Candidate photography

Maintain consistency when appropriate.

# Candidate Assets

Request:

- Headshots
- Action photos
- Family photos
- Event photos
- Logo files
- Previous branding

---

# Opponent Assets

For attack pieces request:

- Headshots
- Public photos
- Public statements
- Supporting documentation

Creative should reinforce documented claims.

---

# Contrast Development

Identify:

## Hero

Who should voters trust?

## Villain

Who should voters reject?

## Conflict

What is the disagreement?

## Consequence

Why should voters care?

A strong mail piece answers all four.

---

# Fact Check Review

Before creative development:

Confirm:

- Sources exist
- Claims are accurate
- Quotes are verified
- Statistics are current
- Legislative records are documented

Weak sourcing weakens strong creative.

---

# Creative Deliverables

Generate:

1. Headline options
2. Subhead options
3. Bullet options
4. Creative concepts
5. Layout recommendations
6. Color recommendations
7. Photo recommendations
8. Contrast recommendations

Do not stop at copy alone.

---

# Creative Concept Requirements

Each concept should include:

## Concept Name

Example:

"Evidence Locker"

## Emotional Goal

Example:

Distrust

## Visual Metaphor

Example:

Investigation

## Headline Direction

Example:

Wrong Choice For Riverside

## Layout Notes

Example:

Photo left, evidence board right

## Strengths

Why it works

Generate multiple concepts whenever possible.

---

# QA Review

Before final recommendation ask:

- Is the message clear in three seconds?
- Is the headline dominant?
- Is the contrast obvious?
- Is the visual tied to the argument?
- Is the body copy readable?
- Are claims sourced?
- Does the piece feel distinct?
- Would this stand out in a stack of mail?

---

# Output Format

Whenever a mailer request is received:

Return:

1. Strategic assessment
2. Recommended contrast frame
3. Headline options
4. Creative concepts
5. Layout recommendation
6. Fact-check concerns
7. Production notes
8. QA observations

Do not jump directly into design.

Think like a strategist first.

Design second.

---

# Default Rule

The strongest mail pieces start with a clear argument, strong contrast, credible facts, and a memorable visual metaphor.

Everything else is secondary.
